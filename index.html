Why it matters

The building is failing.

The Palace of Westminster is in an advanced state of decay. These are the facts.

40,000+
Building failures recorded

Over 40,000 separate incidents of building failures have been recorded since 2017, including electrical faults, water ingress, and structural deterioration.

100+
Years of deferred maintenance

The building has received only reactive, piecemeal maintenance for over a century. Core systems — including Victorian-era pipes and wiring — are far beyond their design lifespan.

High
Fire risk rating

The Palace faces a constant and significant risk of catastrophic fire. Asbestos and outdated building materials are present throughout large sections of the estate.

£[X]m
Additional cost per year of delay

Every year Parliament delays a decision on full restoration, the estimated cost of the eventual works increases by hundreds of millions of pounds.

1834
Last major fire

The previous Palace of Westminster burned down in 1834. The current Victorian building has never undergone comprehensive structural restoration in its 185-year history.

2031+
Earliest possible completion

Even if Parliament voted today to proceed with a full restoration programme, the earliest projected completion date is well into the next decade — and rising.
